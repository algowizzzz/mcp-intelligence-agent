# The Basel Framework

The Basel Framework is the full set of standards of the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS), which is the primary global standard setter for the prudential regulation of banks. The membership of the BCBS has agreed to fully implement these standards and apply them to the internationally active banks in their jurisdictions. The [background page](/baselframework/background.htm) describes the framework's structure and how to navigate it.

### Standards

[SCO](/basel_framework/standard/SCO.htm?tldate=20260403)[Scope and definitions](/basel_framework/standard/SCO.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes the scope of application of the Basel Framework.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2026](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=SCO)[Next version: 01 Jan 2027](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=SCO)Status: Current

[CAP](/basel_framework/standard/CAP.htm?tldate=20260403)[Definition of capital](/basel_framework/standard/CAP.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes the criteria that bank capital instruments must meet to be eligible to satisfy the Basel capital requirements, as well as necessary regulatory adjustments and transitional arrangements.

[Effective as of: 03 Apr 2020](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=CAP)Status: Current

[RBC](/basel_framework/standard/RBC.htm?tldate=20260403)[Risk-based capital requirements](/basel_framework/standard/RBC.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes the framework for risk-based capital requirements.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=RBC)[Next version: 01 Jan 2027](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=RBC)Status: Current

[CRE](/basel_framework/standard/CRE.htm?tldate=20260403)[Calculation of RWA for credit risk](/basel_framework/standard/CRE.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes how to calculate capital requirements for credit risk.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=CRE)[Next version: 01 Jan 2028](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=CRE)Status: Current

[MAR](/basel_framework/standard/MAR.htm?tldate=20260403)[Calculation of RWA for market risk](/basel_framework/standard/MAR.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes how to calculate capital requirements for market risk and credit valuation adjustment risk.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=MAR)Status: Current

[OPE](/basel_framework/standard/OPE.htm?tldate=20260403)[Calculation of RWA for operational risk](/basel_framework/standard/OPE.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes how to calculate capital requirements for operational risk.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=OPE)[Next version: 01 Jan 2027](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=OPE)Status: Current

[LEV](/basel_framework/standard/LEV.htm?tldate=20260403)[Leverage ratio](/basel_framework/standard/LEV.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes the simple, transparent, non-risk-based leverage ratio. This measure intends to restrict the build-up of leverage in the banking sector and reinforce the risk-based requirements with a simple, non-risk-based "backstop" measure.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=LEV)Status: Current

[LCR](/basel_framework/standard/LCR.htm?tldate=20260403)[Liquidity Coverage Ratio](/basel_framework/standard/LCR.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard describes the Liquidity Coverage Ratio, a measure which promotes the short-term resilience of a bank's liquidity risk profile.

[Effective as of: 15 Dec 2019](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=LCR)Status: Current

[NSF](/basel_framework/standard/NSF.htm?tldate=20260403)[Net stable funding ratio](/basel_framework/standard/NSF.htm?tldate=20260403)

The net stable funding ratio requires banks to maintain a stable funding profile in relation to the composition of their assets and off-balance-sheet activities.

[Effective as of: 15 Dec 2019](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=NSF)Status: Current

[LEX](/basel_framework/standard/LEX.htm?tldate=20260403)[Large exposures](/basel_framework/standard/LEX.htm?tldate=20260403)

Large exposures regulation limits the maximum loss that a bank could face in the event of a sudden counterparty failure to a level that does not endanger the bank's solvency. This standard requires banks to measure their exposures to a single counterparty or a group of connected counterparties and limit the size of large exposures in relation to their capital.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2023](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=LEX)Status: Current

[MGN](/basel_framework/standard/MGN.htm?tldate=20260403)[Margin requirements](/basel_framework/standard/MGN.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard establishes minimum standards for margin requirements for non-centrally cleared derivatives. Such requirements reduce systemic risk with respect to non-standardised derivatives by reducing contagion and spillover risks and promoting central clearing.

[Effective as of: 03 Apr 2020](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=MGN)Status: Current

[SRP](/basel_framework/standard/SRP.htm?tldate=20260403)[Supervisory review process](/basel_framework/standard/SRP.htm?tldate=20260403)

The Pillar 2 supervisory review process ensures that banks have adequate capital and liquidity to support all the risks in their business, especially with respect to risks not fully captured by the Pillar 1 process, and encourages good risk management.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2026](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=SRP)Status: Current

[DIS](/basel_framework/standard/DIS.htm?tldate=20260403)[Disclosure requirements](/basel_framework/standard/DIS.htm?tldate=20260403)

This standard sets out disclosure requirements, which aim to encourage market discipline.

[Effective as of: 01 Jan 2026](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=DIS)[Next version: 01 Jan 2027](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=DIS)Status: Current

[BCP](/basel_framework/standard/BCP.htm?tldate=20260403)[Core Principles for effective banking supervision](/basel_framework/standard/BCP.htm?tldate=20260403)

The Basel Core Principles provide a comprehensive standard for establishing a sound foundation for the regulation, supervision, governance and risk management of the banking sector.

[Effective as of: 25 Apr 2024](/basel_framework/timeline.htm?st=BCP)Status: Current
