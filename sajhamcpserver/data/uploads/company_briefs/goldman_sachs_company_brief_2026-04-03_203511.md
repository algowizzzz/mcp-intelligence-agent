## Executive Summary

The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. is a leading global investment banking, securities, and investment management firm headquartered in New York. As a systemically important financial institution (G-SIB), GS operates across investment banking, global markets, asset management, and consumer & wealth management segments. The firm reported strong profitability in 2025 with net income of $12.6B in Q3 2025 and total assets of $1.81 trillion as of September 30, 2025.

## Business Segments

Goldman Sachs operates through four primary business divisions:

1. **Investment Banking** — Advisory services, underwriting, and capital raising
2. **Global Markets** — Trading, sales, and market-making across equities, fixed income, currencies, and commodities
3. **Asset Management** — Investment advisory and portfolio management for institutional and individual clients
4. **Consumer & Wealth Management** — Retail banking, wealth advisory, and consumer lending services

## Key Risk Factors

**Regulatory & Compliance:**
- Resolution plan and recovery plan requirements under Dodd-Frank and international frameworks (BRRD, U.K. Special Resolution Regime)
- Total Loss-Absorbing Capacity (TLAC) compliance for G-SIB designation
- Qualified Financial Contracts (QFC) restrictions limiting default rights and contract transfers
- Cross-jurisdictional complexity across U.S., EU, and U.K. regulatory regimes [src:https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000886982/000088698226000091/gs-20251231.htm]

**Financial & Operational:**
- Debt subject to write-down or conversion into equity under bail-in provisions (BRRD)
- Funding in resolution risk — must maintain adequate liquidity in stressed scenarios
- Trading wind-down capability requirements under U.K. Resolution Assessment Framework
- Contract protocol adherence (ISDA Universal Resolution Stay Protocol) across all qualifying contracts
