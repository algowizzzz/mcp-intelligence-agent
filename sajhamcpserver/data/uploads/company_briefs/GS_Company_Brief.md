## BUSINESS OVERVIEW

The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. is a leading global investment banking, securities, and investment management firm headquartered in New York. GS operates through two primary business segments: Financial Advisory and Underwriting (M&A, capital raising, advisory services) and Trading and Principal Investments (fixed income, equities, commodities, and principal investing). The firm serves institutional clients, corporations, governments, and high-net-worth individuals globally.

## KEY REGULATORY & OPERATIONAL RISKS

**Resolution & Recovery Planning:** GS is subject to Federal Reserve and FDIC resolution plan requirements (next submission due July 1, 2026) and must maintain Total Loss-Absorbing Capacity (TLAC) minimums. [src:https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000886982/000088698226000091/gs-20251231.htm]

**Multi-Jurisdictional Compliance:** GS Bank USA operates under EU Bank Recovery and Resolution Directive (BRRD II) requirements; GSI and GSIB entities comply with UK Special Resolution Regime and Bank of England expectations for loss absorbency and trading wind-down capability.

**Qualified Financial Contract (QFC) Restrictions:** U.S. federal agencies impose restrictions on QFCs requiring counterparties to waive certain default rights, potentially constraining contractual flexibility during stress scenarios.
