## Executive Summary

The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. is a leading global investment banking, securities, and investment management firm headquartered in New York. As a Systemically Important Financial Institution (G-SIB), GS operates across three primary business segments: Financial Advisory and Underwriting, Trading and Principal Investments, and Fixed Income, Currency and Commodities. The firm serves institutional, corporate, and individual clients worldwide.

## Business Segments

Goldman Sachs operates through three primary divisions:

1. **Financial Advisory and Underwriting** — M&A advisory, capital raising, and underwriting services for corporate and institutional clients
2. **Trading and Principal Investments** — Equities and derivatives trading, principal investments, and market-making activities
3. **Fixed Income, Currency and Commodities (FICC)** — Trading and market-making in fixed income, FX, and commodities

## Key Risk Factors

**Regulatory & Compliance:**
- Resolution plan compliance requirements (next GS Bank USA submission due July 1, 2026) [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]
- Global recovery plan requirements and TLAC (Total Loss-Absorbing Capacity) compliance [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]
- EU Bank Recovery and Resolution Directive (BRRD) and UK Special Resolution Regime compliance [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]
- Qualified Financial Contracts (QFC) restrictions under Dodd-Frank [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]

**Financial & Operational:**
- Resolution and bail-in risk affecting unsecured debt holders [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]
- Cross-border subsidiary risk isolation and liquidity constraints [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]
- Operational complexity in maintaining continuity across multiple jurisdictions [src: GS 10-K, 2026-02-25]

## Investment Thesis

Goldman Sachs demonstrated strong profitability in 2025, with full-year EPS growth of 27% and a 16% ROE in Q4 2025. The firm benefits from elevated capital markets activity, robust investment banking demand, and strong trading revenues. However, as a systemically important financial institution, GS faces significant regulatory compliance obligations and resolution planning requirements across multiple jurisdictions. The firm's ability to maintain capital efficiency while meeting enhanced prudential standards remains a key focus area.
