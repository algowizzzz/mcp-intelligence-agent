# Basel III Framework — Overview

**Shared Library — Common Reference Document**

## Summary

Basel III is a global regulatory framework developed by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) following the 2008 financial crisis. It establishes minimum standards for bank capital adequacy, stress testing, and market liquidity risk.

## Key Pillars

### 1. Minimum Capital Requirements
- **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1):** ≥ 4.5% of risk-weighted assets (RWA)
- **Tier 1 Capital:** ≥ 6% of RWA
- **Total Capital Ratio:** ≥ 8% of RWA
- **Capital Conservation Buffer:** 2.5% of RWA (CET1)
- **Countercyclical Buffer:** 0–2.5% of RWA (national discretion)

### 2. Leverage Ratio
- Minimum 3% Tier 1 leverage ratio
- Limits excessive on- and off-balance-sheet leverage

### 3. Liquidity Standards
- **Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR):** ≥ 100% — ensures 30-day survival under stress
- **Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR):** ≥ 100% — stable funding over 1-year horizon

## Market Risk (FRTB)
The Fundamental Review of the Trading Book (FRTB) is the Basel III market risk reform:
- Standardised Approach (SA): sensitivity-based, replaces current Standardised
- Internal Models Approach (IMA): requires P&L attribution test and back-testing
- Internal transfer pricing boundary between banking book and trading book

## Canadian Implementation (OSFI)
OSFI implements Basel III through the Capital Adequacy Requirements (CAR) guideline.
Canadian D-SIBs (Big Six) maintain CET1 ratios well above 11% as of 2025.

## Key Dates
- Basel III phase-in: 2013–2019
- FRTB implementation: January 2025 (most jurisdictions)
- Basel IV (finalised standards): output floors effective 2025–2028
